International Bitterness Units (IBU)

International Bitterness Units (IBU), is defined as the standard measurement of how bitter your beer is based on the hops contained in the drink. According to berghoffbeer.com, IBU "gauges the isomerized alpha acids from hops in a beer in parts per million." It is measured on a scale between 0-100 and found on most alcoholic beverage labels. The higher the count the more bitter tasting the drink will be.

Example IBU for beers:

Budweiser: 7 IBU American Brown Ale: 20 IBU Avery's IPA: 70 IBU Maharaja: 100 IBU
