Alcohol By Volume (ABV)

Alcohol by Volume (ABV or "proof"), is defined as the standard measure of how much alcohol is put in an alcoholic drink. It is measured as a percentage between 0-100% and is found on most alcoholic beverage labels. The higher percentage on the label, the more alcohol is in the drink.

Example ABV for beers:

Bud Light: 4.10% - 4.20% Harp Lager: 5.10% - 5.20% Blue Moon Belgian White: 5.36% - 5.4%
